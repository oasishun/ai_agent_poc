# Trade Regulations

## US Customs & Import Requirements

### ISF (Importer Security Filing)
- Must be filed at least 24 hours before vessel departure from foreign port
- Applies to all ocean freight entering the US
- 10 mandatory data elements required
- Penalty: up to USD 5,000 per violation

### CBP (Customs and Border Protection)
- All shipments to US require advance manifest filing
- AMS (Automated Manifest System) filing required 24h before loading
- ISF-10 filing required for all ocean imports

## Korean Export Regulations

### Export License Requirements
- Dual-use items may require export license from Ministry of Trade
- Controlled goods list updated quarterly
- Electronic export declaration (UNI-PASS system) mandatory

### Cargo Inspection
- Random inspection rate: approximately 3-5% of export shipments
- High-risk shipments subject to 100% inspection
- Inspection at port of export can add 1-3 business days

## Dangerous Goods (DG) Regulations

### IMDG Code Compliance
- All hazardous materials must comply with IMDG Code
- Proper UN number, packing group, and class labeling required
- Carrier acceptance varies by DG class — pre-approval required

### Carrier-Specific DG Restrictions
- HMM: Accepts Class 1-9, restrictions on Class 1 (explosives)
- MAERSK: Full DG acceptance with advance notification
- ONE: Class 7 (radioactive) requires special arrangement

## Reefer Cargo Regulations

### Food Safety Modernization Act (FSMA) — US
- Applies to all food shipments entering the US
- Prior Notice required 8 hours before vessel arrival
- Temperature logs must be maintained throughout transport

### Phytosanitary Requirements
- Fresh produce requires phytosanitary certificate from origin country
- Wood packaging must be ISPM 15 compliant (heat treated or fumigated)
