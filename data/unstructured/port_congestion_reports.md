# Port Congestion Reports

## March 2026 Update

### Los Angeles / Long Beach (USLAX/USLGB)
- **Congestion Level**: MEDIUM
- **Current Wait Time**: 2-4 days for berth availability
- **Root Cause**: Import surge from Asia ahead of peak season
- **Impact**: Average dwell time increased from 72h to 96h
- **Recommendation**: Allow 3-5 extra days for delivery planning

### Shanghai (CNSHA)
- **Congestion Level**: HIGH
- **Current Wait Time**: 3-5 days for transshipment cargo
- **Root Cause**: Post-Chinese New Year volume surge + equipment repositioning
- **Impact**: All transshipment via Shanghai experiencing 2-3 day delays
- **Recommendation**: Prefer direct services or alternative transshipment hubs (CNNGB, CNQIN)

### Busan (KRPUS)
- **Congestion Level**: LOW
- **Current Wait Time**: Less than 24 hours
- **Root Cause**: Normal operations
- **Impact**: Minimal disruption
- **Recommendation**: Normal operations proceed as planned

## Historical Congestion Patterns

### LA/LB Seasonal Patterns
- Peak congestion: August-November (holiday season imports)
- Secondary peak: March-May (Spring imports)
- Low congestion: January-February (post-holiday)

### Shanghai Seasonal Patterns
- Peak congestion: Post-Chinese New Year (February-March)
- Secondary peak: Pre-National Holiday (September)
- Low congestion: June-July, December

## Congestion Mitigation Strategies

1. **Alternative Ports**: Consider Long Beach for LA-bound cargo
2. **Off-peak scheduling**: Target mid-week vessel arrivals
3. **Pre-gate appointments**: Book terminal appointments in advance
4. **Intermodal alternatives**: Rail or truck from alternative ports
5. **Buffer stock**: Increase safety stock during high congestion periods
